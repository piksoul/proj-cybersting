# Workflow - Research Production Process

This workflow documents the production pattern used for **The Satoshi Protocol** ideation phase. It can be reused for other creative projects requiring deep research, strategic planning, and structured development.

---

## Workflow Overview

```
Phase 1: Research Collection
    ↓
Phase 2: Product & Market Analysis
    ↓
Phase 3: Consolidation & Next Steps
    ↓
Phase 4: Core Planning Documents
    ↓
Phase 5: Specifics & Structure
    ↓
Phase 6: Organization & Index
```

---

## Phase 1: Research Collection

**Objective:** Gather comprehensive research across all relevant domains

### Categories to Cover:

#### 1.1 Influences & Inspiration
- **Authors/Creators** - Key influences on style, tone, and approach
- **Works** - Specific books, films, media to study
- **Classics** - Canon reading list for genre/field
- **Successes** - Market analysis of comparable successful works

**Outputs:**
- `research-authors.md` - Author influence analysis
- `research-authors-02.md` - Reading strategy (audio vs physical)
- `research-movies.md` - Film/visual influences
- `research-classics.md` - Canon reading list
- `research-successes.md` - Success case studies

#### 1.2 Thematic & Conceptual Research
- **Philosophical Frameworks** - Core theories and concepts
- **Technical Domains** - Subject matter expertise areas
- **Cultural Elements** - Relevant subcultures and movements
- **Locations** - Settings and geographic research

**Outputs:**
- `research-debord.md` - Philosophical framework (Spectacle theory)
- `research-digital-mythology.md` - Thematic approach
- `research-sotoshi-as-ai.md` - Technical concept analysis
- `research-subcultures.md` - Cultural research overview
- `research-subculture-[specific].md` - Deep dives (breaking, tresor, etc.)
- `research-locations.md` - Geographic/setting research

#### 1.3 Format & Medium Exploration
- **Alternative Formats** - Different ways to tell the story
- **Serialization Options** - Publishing strategies
- **Experimental Approaches** - Innovative formats
- **Medium-Specific** - Audio, interactive, web-based, etc.

**Outputs:**
- `research-formats.md` - Format options overview
- `research-serialisation.md` - Serialization analysis
- `research-format-audio-first.md` - Audio-specific approach
- `research-format-experimental.md` - Experimental formats
- `research-format-interactive.md` - Interactive fiction
- `research-format-web-world.md` - Web-based narrative

#### 1.4 Strategic Research
- **Approach & Method** - How to conduct research effectively
- **Authorship Strategy** - Pseudonym vs real name
- **Safety & Guidelines** - Legal/ethical boundaries

**Outputs:**
- `research-approach.md` - Hybrid development strategy
- `research-authorship.md` - Authorship strategy analysis
- `research-clear-zones.md` - Safety guidelines

### Phase 1 Success Criteria:
- [ ] All major influence areas documented
- [ ] Thematic frameworks researched
- [ ] Format options explored
- [ ] Strategic considerations addressed
- [ ] 15-25 research documents created

---

## Phase 2: Product & Market Analysis

**Objective:** Understand market positioning and product strategy

### 2.1 Product Strategy
Define the product(s) you're creating:
- Primary format (novel, graphic novel, etc.)
- Secondary formats (adaptations, companion pieces)
- Format-specific focus areas
- Execution strategy for multiple formats

**Output:**
- `product-strategy.md` - Development strategy for all formats

### 2.2 Competitive Analysis
Analyze the market landscape:
- Direct competitors
- Adjacent markets
- Unique positioning
- Market gaps and opportunities
- Target audiences

**Output:**
- `product-competition.md` - Market analysis and positioning

### Phase 2 Success Criteria:
- [ ] Product strategy defined
- [ ] Market position identified
- [ ] Competitive advantages clear
- [ ] Target audiences mapped
- [ ] 2-3 strategy documents created

---

## Phase 3: Consolidation & Next Steps

**Objective:** Synthesize research into actionable options

### 3.1 Next Steps Identification
Review all research and identify:
- Potential development directions
- Character/world building opportunities
- Structural options
- Format experiments to try
- Thematic integrations possible

**Process:**
1. Read through all research docs
2. Extract "next steps" or "opportunities" sections
3. Organize by category:
   - Story & Character Development
   - Structure & Outline
   - Worldbuilding & Lore
   - Thematic Integration
   - Format Experiments
   - Production & Planning

**Output:**
- `next-steps.md` - Comprehensive menu of development options
- `next-steps-[##].md` - Additional collections as needed

### Phase 3 Success Criteria:
- [ ] All research synthesized
- [ ] Options organized by category
- [ ] Clear development paths identified
- [ ] 1-2 next-steps documents created

---

## Phase 4: Core Planning Documents

**Objective:** Create execution-ready strategic artifacts

### 4.1 Idea Recap
Comprehensive assessment of current status:
- **Core Story Concept** - Current form of the premise
- **Strongest Elements** - What's working exceptionally well
- **Weak Spots / Gaps** - What needs development
- **Opportunities** - Where you can expand
- **Overall Status** - Foundation assessment

**Output:**
- `idea-recap.md` - Complete status assessment

### 4.2 Agentic Brief
High-level workflow brief for AI/agent development:
- Story summary (elevator pitch version)
- Genre and comparables
- Unique selling points
- Key selling points
- AI-agentic workflow notes (if applicable)
- Development pipeline structure

**Output:**
- `agentic-brief.md` - Agent workflow brief

### 4.3 One-Pager
Professional pitch document:
- Working title
- Genre and tone
- Length goal
- **Logline** (1-2 sentences)
- **Premise Summary** (200-250 words)
- **The Hook** (memorable key moment)

**Output:**
- `one-pager.md` - Professional pitch page

### Phase 4 Success Criteria:
- [ ] Current status clearly assessed
- [ ] Strengths and gaps identified
- [ ] Professional pitch ready
- [ ] Agent workflow defined (if applicable)
- [ ] 3 core planning documents created

---

## Phase 5: Specifics & Structure

**Objective:** Develop detailed structural and creative specifics

### 5.1 Narrative Design
Specific to creative projects:
- **Pacing** - Tension/stakes visualization
- **Guidelines** - How to integrate concepts naturally
- **Philosophical Depth** - Thematic framework
- **Premise** - High-level concept
- **Key Scenes** - Signature moments

**Outputs:**
- `narrative-pacing.md` - Pacing structure
- `narrative-guidelines.md` - Writing guidelines
- `narrative-existentialism.md` - Philosophical framework
- `narrative-concept-premise.md` - Premise document
- `narrative-concept-scene-[##].md` - Key scene breakdowns

### 5.2 Structural Outline
Detailed chapter/section structure:
- Full chapter tree (for novels)
- Act structure
- Beat sheets
- Scene breakdowns
- Structural pacing

**Output:**
- `chapter-tree.md` - Complete structural outline

### 5.3 Domain-Specific Documents
Additional specifics based on project type:
- For **business projects**: financial models, go-to-market
- For **technical projects**: architecture, specs
- For **creative projects**: character sheets, world bible
- For **research projects**: methodology, frameworks

**Outputs:**
- Varies by project type

### Phase 5 Success Criteria:
- [ ] Narrative approach defined
- [ ] Structure outlined in detail
- [ ] Key scenes/moments identified
- [ ] Domain-specific docs created
- [ ] 5-10 specifics documents created

---

## Phase 6: Organization & Index

**Objective:** Create navigation and organization systems

### 6.1 README/Index Creation
Master navigation document:
- Document organization by type
- Document count by category
- Alternative organization suggestions
- Document types reference
- Workflow recommendations
- Cross-reference system

**Output:**
- `README.md` - Master index and navigation

### 6.2 Active Tracking
Ongoing work management:
- Task lists
- Current priorities
- Blockers and issues
- Timeline (if applicable)

**Output:**
- `todo.md` - Active task tracking

### Phase 6 Success Criteria:
- [ ] All documents indexed
- [ ] Navigation clear and accessible
- [ ] Categories well-organized
- [ ] Tracking system in place
- [ ] 2 organizational documents created

## Timeline Recommendations

### Intensive Research Mode (2-4 weeks):
- Week 1: Phase 1a (Influences) + Phase 1b (Thematic)
- Week 2: Phase 1c (Format) + Phase 1d (Strategic) + Phase 2
- Week 3: Phase 3 + Phase 4
- Week 4: Phase 5 + Phase 6

### Parallel Development Mode (4-8 weeks):
- Weeks 1-3: Phase 1 (one category per week)
- Week 4: Phase 2
- Week 5: Phase 3 + Phase 4
- Week 6-7: Phase 5
- Week 8: Phase 6

### Ongoing Research Mode (8-12 weeks):
- Continuous Phase 1 research alongside development
- Rolling Phase 3 consolidation weekly
- Phase 4-5 development in parallel
- Phase 6 ongoing maintenance

## Adaptation for Different Project Types

### For Business/Startup Projects:
- **Phase 1:** Market research, competitor analysis, user research, technical research
- **Phase 2:** Business model, market positioning
- **Phase 3:** Strategic options
- **Phase 4:** Business plan, pitch deck, executive summary
- **Phase 5:** Financial models, go-to-market, product specs
- **Phase 6:** Documentation hub, roadmap

### For Technical/Research Projects:
- **Phase 1:** Literature review, technology research, methodology research
- **Phase 2:** Application domains, existing solutions
- **Phase 3:** Research questions and approaches
- **Phase 4:** Research proposal, hypothesis, objectives
- **Phase 5:** Methodology, architecture, experimental design
- **Phase 6:** Documentation, bibliography, tracking

### For Creative Projects (Current Template):
- **Phase 1:** Influences, themes, formats, subcultures, locations
- **Phase 2:** Product strategy, market analysis
- **Phase 3:** Development options
- **Phase 4:** Idea recap, pitch, brief
- **Phase 5:** Narrative design, structure, specifics
- **Phase 6:** Index, tracking

## Reusability Notes

This workflow has been validated on **The Satoshi Protocol** (creative novel project).

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive research foundation
- Clear progression from research → execution
- Multiple synthesis points (consolidation, recap, pitch)
- Scalable to different project sizes
- Adaptable to different domains

**When to Use:**
- Large, complex creative projects
- Projects requiring deep research
- Projects with multiple format options
- Projects needing strategic positioning
- Projects with philosophical/thematic depth

**When NOT to Use:**
- Simple, single-purpose projects
- Projects with tight timelines (<2 weeks)
- Projects where execution is more important than research
- Maintenance/iterative work on existing projects
