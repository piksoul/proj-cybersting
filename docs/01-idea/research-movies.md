### Style:
- Con artistry as an art form
- Elaborate long cons with multi-layered deception
- Chess-like plotting where characters mislead both enemies and the audience

### Key Themes/Appeal:
- Trust, betrayal, manipulation

### Scenario Fit for Your Novel:

You can use The Sting influence to design:
- A long con woven through the entire plot (the sabotage of the Satoshi keys)
- A mastermind (mentor, saboteur, or rogue group) who set up the "switch" years in advance
- Layered misdirection, where the U.S. thinks it controls the keys but the con was played on them
- Elias discovering key parts of the con piece by piece
- A final "reveal" where we see the entire con assembled (flashbacks + montage)

**Essentially: The Satoshi-key sabotage is a global long con at the scale of The Sting.**

## ðŸŽ¬ 4. The Tailor of Panama (2001)

### Style:
- Spycraft meets farce
- Intelligence lies, counterlies, and invented "intel" spiralling out of control
- Manipulation, fabricated narratives
- Dark comedy and satire blended with espionage
- A con that becomes geopolitical disaster

### Key Themes/Appeal:
- How disinformation becomes reality
- How intelligence agencies believe what they want to believe
- Ordinary people trapped in systems they don't fully understand

### Scenario Fit for Your Novel:

This is spot-on for the dynamic in your Satoshi-keys plot:
- Bureaucrats believe they control something they never did
- Internal political stupidity accelerates a global crisis
- Elias becomes an "unreliable narrator" to power
- Intelligence factions spin fake crypto intel
- A comedic beat where bureaucrats try the Satoshi keys and nothing works
- A sense of tragic absurdity: "The world is run by people who don't understand the systems that run the world."

**This film gives your story its dark humour, bureaucracy satire, and intelligence-community absurdity.**
