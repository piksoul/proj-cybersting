## B. Cyberpunk Elements (Surveillance, AI, Darknet, rogue code)

Blend into setting and character experience:

- Describe everyday spaces saturated with sensors
- Highlight "digital shadows" tracking characters
- Darknet comms with eerie minimal UIs
- Subtle hints of non-human behaviour in code commits
- Speculative but grounded tech (drones, spoofed biometrics, neural hashes)

## C. Early Bitcoin / Satoshi Mystery

Reveal piece-by-piece to maintain mystery:

- **Act I:** anomalies, patterns
- **Act II:** conspiracies, internal factions, rogue researchers
- **Act III:** the truth, ambiguous and philosophical

**Rules:**
- Never explain everything
- Keep the mythos deeper than the reader's access
- Let characters disagree on what's real

## D. Political Intrigue & Multipolar Strategy

Show, don't tell:

- Russia conducts a surgical operation in Zurich
- China deploys MSS "economic security" units
- EU splits under pressure
- Middle Eastern sovereign funds quietly hedge BTC exposure
- IMF & BIS panic quietly behind closed doors

Everything is a response to the Satoshi keys failing.

## E. Human Drama & Betrayals

Anchor the macro in micro:

- Betrayal by an ally
- Mentor who knew only fragments of the truth
- Conflicts inside U.S. agencies
- Elias' guilt and idealism clashing with realpolitik

**Human stakes keep the tech digestible.**

### 1. Basic Cryptographic Principles

**Objective:** make cryptography intuitive, not academic.

**Sprinkle-in Moments:**
- Elias explaining signatures using a lockbox metaphor during a chase
- A schoolteacher flashback
- A hacker demonstrating a one-way function using physical objects
- Visual metaphors (keys, shards, mirrors, fractals)

### 2. Proof-of-Work Mining

**Objective:** show mining as physics, not magic.

**Sprinkle-in Moments:**
- A mining farm scene with intense heat + sound
- A character likening mining to "lifting weights for the network"
- Power infrastructure sabotage stakes
- A rogue mining pool acting as a geopolitical actor

### 3. Decentralisation

**Objective:** illustrate decentralisation as resilience, not ideology.

**Sprinkle-in Moments:**
- A node running in unexpected places (a fishing boat, a basement)
- Conversations about trust vs institutions
- Graphical depiction of network maps
- Failures of centralised systems as contrasts

### 4. Financial Literacy

**Objective:** subtly teach without lecturing.

**Sprinkle-in Moments:**
- Characters reacting differently to inflation, debt, or risk
- Flashback to Elias helping someone understand compound interest
- A villain misusing financial jargon â€” revealing their ignorance
- Quick metaphors: *"Currencies are like storiesâ€¦ some are lies told better."*

### 5. Sovereignty (Individual & National)

**Objective:** treat sovereignty as a philosophical battleground.

**Sprinkle-in Moments:**
- Characters debating who should control money
- Scenes where centralized power is abused
- Elias confronting his own lack of personal sovereignty
- A nation-state's desperation to escape the USD system

### 6. Range of Novel Technologies

**Objective:** showcase believable near-future tech without sci-fi overstretch.

**Sprinkle-in Moments:**
